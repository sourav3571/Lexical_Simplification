========================================================================
                     DETAILED STAGE JUSTIFICATION
========================================================================

1. PREPROCESSING
   Uses spaCy to parse POS tags and extract nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and
   proper nouns. This ensures grammatical correctness and ignores function words.

2. CONTEXT-AWARE CWI (COMPLEX WORD IDENTIFICATION)
   Uses a 100% BERT-driven, POS-independent model:
   - Inherent Bias (30%): Extracted from MLM output layer biases (correlated with
     general frequency) with a subword token length penalty.
   - Embedding Drift (50%): Measures cosine distance between context embedding and
     a concrete reference template ("I see the [word]."). Distinguishes abstract,
     figurative senses (high drift, complex) from concrete senses (low drift, simple).
   - Prediction Spread (20%): Evaluates top-10 prediction entropy at the word mask,
     capturing lexical and syntactic uncertainty.
   - Dynamic Threshold: Flags a word as complex if it exceeds (mean + 1.0 * std_dev)
     or falls in the top 25% of content words in the sentence (minimum baseline: 0.33).

3. WORD SENSE DISAMBIGUATION (WSD)
   Matches the context embedding of the target word with definition sentence
   embeddings of all WordNet synsets. The highest cosine similarity determines
   the exact semantic sense.

4. CANDIDATE GENERATION & FILTERING
   Aggregates raw candidates from WordNet synsets, hypernyms, hyponyms, sister terms,
   and BERT MLM. Filters candidates using:
   - Inherent Simplicity: Candidate inherent complexity must not exceed target word
     complexity + 0.15.
   - Semantic Relation: Candidate must be semantically linked in WordNet synsets.
   - Grammatical / Contextual Fit: BERT MLM probability at the mask >= 0.0005.
   - Meaning Preservation: Sentence embedding similarity (orig vs cand) > 0.85.

5. CONTEXTUAL NEURAL RANKER
   Applies a trained PyTorch Gated Fusion network (`best_model.pt`) that scores
   each candidate based on four neural features: MLM probability, contextual word
   cosine similarity, surprisal reduction, and sentence fluency change.

6. 4-GATE BERT VALIDATION
   Individually verifies candidates in order of their rank score. A candidate is
   only accepted if it passes all four gates:
   - Gate 1 (MLM Pref): Candidate probability is higher than original, or >= 0.001.
   - Gate 2 (Similarity): Sentence cosine sim > 0.85 AND word-level cosine sim > 0.65.
   - Gate 3 (Fluency): Fluency score does not drop by more than 0.7 log-loss units.
   - Gate 4 (Complexity Reduction): Contextual complexity score of the candidate
     in the new sentence must be lower than that of the original word.

7. PARALLEL SUBSTITUTION
   Replaces all accepted words at once using a parallel replacer, adjusting casing
   (capitalization) dynamically, while avoiding index-shifting cascading bugs.
